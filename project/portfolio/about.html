Basic Rules

In each round of blackjack, players sitting at the table start by placing a bet in their respective betting positions, also known as "boxes". Each table clearly establishes the minimum and maximum bet that each player can bet, and the sizes of the bets can be chosen by clicking on the chip icon that represents the correct value of a desired bet. After placing all bets, players will receive two cards face up in front of their boxes, and the bank will receive a card (European style) or two cards with one up and one down (Atlantic City style), depending on the variant that is playing

The goal of any blackjack hand is to defeat the bank. For this, you must have a hand that scores higher than the hand of the bank, but does not exceed 21 points in total value. Or, you can win with a score below 22 when the hand of the bank exceeds 21 points. When the total value of your hand is 22 or more, this is commonly known as "bankruptcy," and you will automatically lose any money wagered.
